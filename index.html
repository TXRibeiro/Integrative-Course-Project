About the Project

Critical infrastructure for resilient cities

The Disaster-Ready Water Storage System is a multidisciplinary project focused on developing resilient water storage solutions for urban contexts in natural disaster or emergency situations. We combine electronic engineering, software, geographic analysis, and resource management to create an integrated, monitored, and scalable system.

The Problem

Why cities are vulnerable

Growing War Risk

Armed conflicts target water infrastructure as strategic objectives, compromising urban supply.

Imminent Seismic Activity

Earthquakes damage distribution networks and reservoirs, suddenly disrupting access to drinking water.

Climate Change Droughts

Increasingly frequent drought episodes reduce water availability and overload supply systems.

Compromise of Main Reservoirs

Failures in central reservoirs leave entire cities without supply alternatives in emergency situations.

The Core Problem

In an emergency situation, access to drinking water in the first 72h is crucial for population survival and the functioning of essential services.

Public Health Impact
Emergency Services
Critical Infrastructure

Pathogenic Contamination

High biological risk contaminations compromise drinking water safety and represent a serious threat to public health.

Implementation Phases

System behavior in two scenarios

Normal Operation

  1. Strategic installation of modules
  2. Integration into the municipal water network
  3. Monitor:
    • Water level
    • Water quality
  4. Define maintenance protocols
  5. Inter-module communication and with database under normal conditions
  6. Real-time website for normal conditions

Disaster

  1. Immediate availability in critical time
  2. Monitor water level
  3. Control access and limits
  4. Ensure system resilience, even in case of power and telecommunications failure
  5. Modularity and transport to most affected areas
